Strategies to Reduce Food Waste

Smart Shopping

  • Make a shopping list and only buy the food you need.

  • Check the expiration date carefully while shopping and choose the food that expires the soonest.

  • Buy fresh produce from local farmers' markets whenever possible to reduce food waste during transportation.

  • Choose misshapen or "ugly" fruits and vegetables that are just as nutritious as their "perfect" counterparts.

  • Buy in bulk and store food properly to reduce packaging waste.

Proper Refrigeration

  • Store food at the correct temperature in the refrigerator and freezer to prevent spoilage.

  • Label and date leftovers and place them in the front of the refrigerator to remind yourself to eat them before they expire.

  • Use clear containers to store leftovers and keep them organized.

  • Store fruits and vegetables separately to prevent them from releasing gases that can cause spoilage.

  • Keep your refrigerator clean and free of spoiled food to prevent contamination.

Utilizing Leftovers

  • Create new meals from leftovers, such as using leftover rice to make fried rice or using leftover vegetables to make soup.

  • Freeze leftovers for future use, such as making a soup or stew.

  • Use leftover bread to make croutons or breadcrumbs.

  • Make a meal plan to use up leftovers before they expire.

  • Compost any food scraps that cannot be consumed or used.
